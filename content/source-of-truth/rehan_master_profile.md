# Dr. Rehan Mahmood

## Title Line
Associate Professor | Director of SSTRL | CubeSat / Lunar Mission / Satellite Communications

## Executive Biography
Dr. Rehan Mahmood is an aerospace systems engineer, academic, and project director based in Pakistan, with over 20 years of experience in satellite technology and communication engineering. He is Director and Principal Investigator of the Small Satellite Technology and Research Lab (SSTRL) and Associate Professor at the Institute of Space Technology (IST), Islamabad.

He is one of three Principal Investigators for ICUBE-Q, Pakistan's lunar CubeSat mission aboard Chang'e-6, with leadership contributions across ICUBE-1, SSS-2A, ICUBE-N, ICUBE-CSAT, and mission-oriented STEM initiatives. He currently leads indigenous 3U CubeSat and CubeSat training-kit development through SSTRL in collaboration with private partners. Current research and development includes NGN/NTN–PPDR digital twin, resilient communications, and policy-oriented simulation directions.

## Key Projects and Missions
### ICUBE-Q
ICUBE-Q is Pakistan's lunar CubeSat mission and flew aboard China's Chang'e-6 mission. The lunar orbital opportunity was opened to APSCO member states, Pakistan's proposal and ICUBE-Q team were selected, and Dr. Rehan Mahmood served as one of three Principal Investigators. As one of the payloads associated with the Chang'e-6 mission, ICUBE-Q contributed to Pakistan's visible lunar-mission capability and public space-technology credibility.

### ICUBE-1
ICUBE-1 was Pakistan's first CubeSat and launched on 21 November 2013. Dr. Rehan Mahmood served in a project-management and senior systems-engineering role, contributing across subsystem/software work, environmental testing, launch-campaign participation, and broader mission execution support. The mission represents a foundational step in Pakistan's CubeSat capability development.

### SSS-2A
SSS-2A is part of APSCO's Student Small Satellite (SSS) program, which includes SSS-1, SSS-2A, and SSS-2B. From the Pakistan side, Dr. Rehan Mahmood served as the technical lead / expert and worked on ADCS, power, and communications in collaboration with Shanghai Jiao Tong University. The program reflects structured international small-satellite collaboration and student-oriented capability development.

### ICUBE-N
ICUBE-N reflects national CubeSat capability-building with current emphasis on indigenous architecture, subsystem development, and mission-oriented nanosatellite engineering through SSTRL and associated programs.

### ICUBE-CSAT / KidSat
Directed classroom satellite and STEM initiatives designed to bring mission-based space engineering into education.
