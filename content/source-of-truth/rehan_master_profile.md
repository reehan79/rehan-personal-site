# Dr. Rehan Mahmood

## Title Line
Associate Professor | Director of SSTRL | CubeSat / Lunar Mission / Satellite Communications

## Executive Biography
Dr. Rehan Mahmood is an aerospace systems engineer, academic, and project director based in Pakistan, with over 20 years of experience in satellite technology and communication engineering. He is Director and Principal Investigator of the Small Satellite Technology and Research Lab (SSTRL) and Associate Professor at the Institute of Space Technology (IST), Islamabad.

He is widely recognized for his role in Pakistan's lunar CubeSat mission ICUBE-Q, launched aboard Chang'e-6, and for sustained contributions to small-satellite capability development through ICUBE-1, SSS-2A, ICUBE-N, and ICUBE-CSAT. His current work also includes NGN/NTN–PPDR digital-twin and scenario-evaluation research, linking space systems with resilient communications and telecom-space convergence.

## Key Projects and Missions
### ICUBE-Q
Pakistan's lunar CubeSat mission launched aboard Chang'e-6. Served as one of three Principal Investigators and contributed to mission-oriented execution, payload integration, and national visibility in lunar small-satellite capability.

### ICUBE-1
Pakistan's first university CubeSat. Worked on project management, subsystem analysis, software, testing, launch campaign participation, and post-launch operations.

### SSS-2A
APSCO Student Small Satellite-2A developed with Shanghai Jiao Tong University. Served as the Pakistan-side technical expert / lead in areas linked to EPS, communications, and systems coordination.

### ICUBE-N
Project Director for the National CubeSat of Pakistan, with current emphasis on nanosatellite architecture and subsystem capability building.

### ICUBE-CSAT / KidSat
Directed classroom satellite and STEM initiatives designed to bring mission-based space engineering into education.
